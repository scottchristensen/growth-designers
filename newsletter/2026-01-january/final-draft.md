# One A/B Test, Two Opposite Results

**Source:** [growth-designers.beehiiv.com](https://growth-designers.beehiiv.com/p/one-a-b-test-two-opposite-results)
**Published:** January 2026

---

This article presents a compelling case study comparing identical A/B tests conducted at two fintech companies with starkly different outcomes.

## Mercury's Success

Andy Hsu at Mercury tested converting a deactivated button to an active state with error handling. The result was impressive—"20.2% increased click-through rate." This led Mercury to deprecate all deactivated buttons company-wide.

## Prosper's Flat Results

The author attempted the same test at Prosper on their loan application and found "no or insignificant change," choosing to maintain their original button pattern.

## Core Message

Even though Mercury and Prosper ended up with opposite results, both experiments were still valuable.

"When communities...share what actually worked and what fell flat, we all get better at designing the right tests for our own users instead of chasing someone else's outlier."

## Why It Matters

This demonstrates why direct peer validation matters more than secondhand success stories, especially in an AI era where information spreads rapidly. Testing hypotheses within your specific context, rather than blindly adopting others' "wins," remains essential for meaningful optimization.

The takeaway: replicate promising tests in your own environment before full implementation.
